Airplane Configurator

The project helps sales and marketing team of a leading aircraft manufacturer in USA to set up and configure airplane interiors providing various options such as quantity of Seats (Business, First and Economy), type of Lavatories, Galleys, Video Controllers etc., while taking orders from airline customers. This application helps to check cabin layouts to ensure compliance with physical constraints, certification requirements and corporate design policies. It gives a provision to create 2D and 3D product configurations with interactive, drag-and-drop technology and extending it to virtual reality and 3D walkthroughs of Cabin Layouts. The project helped in improving digital customer experience by enhancing the visual representation of commercial airplane interiors configurations from traditional paper-based drawings and PDF catalogs.


Note : In order to maintain confidentiality, client name has not been disclosed. All UI and data are mocked samples from actual implementation.

Configuring Airplane 2D

The customer specific airplane configuration helps the airliners and manufacturer to make faster decisions, elevate customer experience, incorporate engineering re-use and reduce cost for managing configurations with an easy to use drag and drop based interactive interface.

I am the front developer to create the 2D interface module. The application is developed in WPF leveraging WPF canvas and XAML paths to create the 2D with high degree of customization. Configuring such a product required lot of iteration, user interviews, user feedback and to understand the human factors associated with the use case. My role at onsite demanded a lot of interaction with the business users rather than just developing the UI.

3D Walkthrough & Review

3D walkthroughs help the customers to take-home plug and play cabin experience. Realtime changes made in 2D are visualized back in 3D, which accelerates the review process.

My role in this module is create the low fidelity 3D objects from PLM sources which later gets converted to high fidelity graphics by a 3rd party organization for each commodity and options. I was also part of the end to end development of option authoring module where the OEM engineers can add/modify/delete options from their catalog including the associated 3D data.This helps the configurations offered by the OEM to be version controlled, so the that customer can choose from the right avilable options

Mixed Reality

Supported by Microsoft MR experts and engineers, OEM and our team were exploring how HoloLens headsets can be used to give airline buyers a better understanding of the appearance and spatial quality of the cabin, moving customers from merely seeing to fully experiencing aircraft configurations and assessing the impacts of their selections in real time.

In this process I am part of the team helping the end to end conversion of the CAD models to high fidelity VR based models through automations. I am also involved in MR development using visual studio, universal windows platform, Hololens and unity.
